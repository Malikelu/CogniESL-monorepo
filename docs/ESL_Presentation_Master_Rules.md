# Master Rules: AI-Generated ESL Presentations

## 1. Objective & Core Philosophy

This document defines the strict pedagogical and structural rules for an AI agent tasked with generating English as a Second Language (ESL) grammar presentations. The goal is to move beyond generic "lecture" slides toward a Modular PPP (Presentation, Practice, Production) framework that targets specific linguistic interference (L1 Oracle).

---

## 2. Visual & Formatting Standards (The "Anti-Fatigue" Layer)

### The 80/20 Visual Rule

Every slide MUST maintain a ratio of 80% visual/white space to 20% text. High-quality imagery is mandatory for contextualization.

### The 6x6 Text Rule

No slide shall exceed 6 words per line or 6 lines of text. This prevents cognitive overload for language learners.

### Typography

Use only clean, professional Sans-Serif fonts (e.g., Arial, Montserrat, Roboto).

### Teacher "Cheat Sheets"

Every slide MUST include 2-3 lines of "Teacher Talk" in the Speaker Notes to guide the instructor.

---

## 3. Modular Expansion Logic (The "Chunking" Rule)

The presentation length MUST be dynamic based on the topic's complexity. The AI must never cram multiple rules onto one slide.

| Presentation Type | Total Slides | Requirement |
|-------------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Standard Topic** (e.g., Pronouns) | 8–12 Slides | Single branch, minimal mechanic rules |
| **Complex Topic** (e.g., Simple Present) | 15–25 Slides | Requires separate modules for "Be," "Action Verbs," and "Spelling" |
| **Foundational Topic** (e.g., Parts of Speech) | 20+ Slides | A dedicated module for every category (Nouns, Verbs, etc.) |

### The "Rule of One"

Each individual grammar branch or spelling rule (e.g., -s vs -es vs -ies) requires its own dedicated slide.

---

## 4. The Sectional Hierarchy (The Flow)

### Section 1: The Engagement Hook (1–2 Slides)

- **Slide 1: Visual Lead-in** - A high-impact image that creates a "puzzle" related to the topic
- **Slide 2: Contextualization** - Use speech bubbles to show target grammar being used naturally by characters in the image

### Section 2: Presentation & Meaning (2–5 Slides)

- **The Logic (CCQs):** Use Concept Check Questions to verify understanding before showing formulas (e.g., "Is it happening now?")
- **The Visual Equation:** Present formulas as equations: Subject + Be + Verb-ing. Use color-coding for parts of speech

### Section 3: Technical Mechanics (2–6 Slides)

- **Spelling & Irregulars:** Dedicated slides for each rule variant
- **Phonetic Guide:** Highlight contractions (I'm, You're) and specific phonetic hurdles

### Section 4: The L1 Oracle (1–2 Slides)

- **The "Ghost Error" Shield:** Dedicate space to highlight mistakes specific to the students' native language
- **Example (L1 Spanish):** Contrast "Is raining" (X) vs "It is raining" (Check)

### Section 5: Scaffolded Practice (3–6 Slides)

- **Controlled:** High-restriction gap-fills or multiple-choice
- **Semi-Controlled:** Prompts requiring personal answers (e.g., "Look out the window. What is happening?")

### Section 6: Production & Wrap-up (2–3 Slides)

- **The Task/Game:** A communicative activity (Role-play, "Find Someone Who") where the grammar is the tool to finish a goal
- **The Carrot:** A final summary "Cheat Sheet" slide for the students to take home
