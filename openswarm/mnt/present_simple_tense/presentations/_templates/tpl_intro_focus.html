Present Simple: Course Focus

This lesson explores the present simple tense for habits, routines, and general truths in everyday English. It is built for Portuguese-speaking adults who need practical, confident communication.

Primary Learning Goal

Use present simple structures accurately in real daily situations, not only in isolated grammar drills.

Students connect form, meaning, and context so they can choose this tense naturally while speaking and writing. Explanations stay clear, while practice stays focused on communication outside the classroom.

Practical language first, with grammar supporting communication.

Portuguese-to-English contrast to prevent translation errors.

Interactive repetition to convert understanding into active use.

Habits and Daily Routines

Learners describe repeated actions such as commuting, meals, and work schedules using stable present simple patterns.

Because the contexts are familiar, adults can transfer these structures directly to small talk and workplace conversations.

General Truths and Facts

The lesson clarifies when present simple communicates ideas that are generally true, stable, or factual.

This distinction reduces tense confusion and helps learners sound precise when explaining information.

Portuguese ↔ English Comparison

Portuguese-speaking adults compare structures directly to identify where English verb forms shift by subject.

This contrastive approach reduces literal translation and supports faster self-correction during conversation.

Interactive Practice for Real Use

Guided dialogue and role-play tasks push students to use present simple in real-time speaking exchanges.

Immediate feedback links grammar choices to clear outcomes, building confidence for everyday interactions.
